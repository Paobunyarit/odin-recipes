Ingredient

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, or more as needed
  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 3 cooked boneless skinless chicken breast halves, shredded
  • 1¾ cup minced onion
  • ¼ cup butter
  • 2¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 (4 ounce) can chopped green chiles, drained
  • ½ cup chopped green onions
  • ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro

Direction

step 1

Gather all ingredients.

step 2

Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.

step 3

Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Fry tortillas (one at a time)
for 5 seconds on each side to soften and make them pliable. Add more oil to the pan as needed.

step 4

Drain between layers of paper towel and keep warm.

step 5

Divide chicken, 10 ounces Monterey Jack cheese, and onion among 12 tortillas

step 6

Roll up each tortilla and place seam-side down in the prepared pan.

step 7

Melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add flour and whisk until mixture begins
to boil. Slowly add chicken broth, stirring with a whisk until thickened. Mix in sour cream and green chiles,
stirring occasionally. Heat thoroughly but do not boil.

step 8

Pour mixture over enchiladas.

step 9

Bake in the preheated oven until bubbly and heated through, about 20 minutes. Top with
remaining Monterey Jack cheese and bake for 5 more minutes.

step 10

Garnish with chopped green onions and cilantro.
